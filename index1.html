YouTube Laws: How to Start Seriously and Not Waste Your Time

Most YouTube guides are fluff. This set of laws is what’s really important and necessary if you want to survive on the platform.

Law I — SEO & Thumbnails

Essence: SEO opens the way to your video, but doesn’t make it good.

Important: Titles, tags, thumbnails, keyword variations.

Flip side: Overdoing tags/text reduces CTR; duplicating title on thumbnail annoys viewers.

Example: “CS2: Top 5 Unreal Moments!” — tags: CS2 top moments, CS2 highlights, CS2 fail.

Law II — Don’t Rely Only on SEO

SEO brings viewers, but only interesting content keeps them.

Flip side: a good video can still succeed even with imperfect SEO.

Law III — Video Length

Videos under 8 minutes rarely get promoted, ideal ~10 minutes.

Don’t stretch content unnaturally — viewers notice.

Flip side: long but empty videos reduce retention.

Law IV — Consistency

Minimum 2 videos per month. Prepare in advance: editing, backup videos.

Flip side: too much posting without quality wastes viewers’ time.

Law V — Final Review

Watch the full video before posting. 80% script, 20% improv. Edit: remove unnecessary parts, maintain pace.

Flip side: empty pauses kill engagement.

Law VI — Define Your Format

Choose format: entertainment, educational, business. Your delivery should match.

Flip side: trying to be “everything at once” loses audience.
